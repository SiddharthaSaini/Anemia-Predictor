About

Anemia:

Low red blood cells or hemoglobin, causing oxygen shortage.

Symptoms:

fatigue, weakness, shortness of breath, pale skin.

Causes:

nutritional deficiencies, diseases, blood loss.

Treatments:

vary.

Hemoglobin

Hemoglobin, a protein in red blood cells, transports oxygen and carbon dioxide.

Healthy ranges: Men: 13.2–16.6 g/dL, Women: 11.6–15 g/dL.

MCH

An MCH value refers to the average quantity of hemoglobin present in a single red blood cell.

Normal range: 27.5–33.2 pg.

MCHC

The mean corpuscular hemoglobin concentration is average concentration of hemoglobin in red blood cells.

Normal range: 33.4–35.5 g/dL.

MCV

MCV stands for mean corpuscular volume. An MCV blood test measures the average size of red blood cells.

Normal range: 80–100 fL.
